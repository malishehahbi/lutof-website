# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Colorists, video editors, and photographers who work with large libraries of color grading LUTs. They are typically time-constrained professionals working in dim edit bays or studios, looking up from their timeline to reference or download tools. They value speed, accuracy, and interfaces that stay out of the way.

## Product Purpose

Lutof is a desktop application for previewing, comparing, and managing color grading LUTs. The website's job is to communicate that value instantly and drive downloads. Every design decision must serve clarity and conversion — no filler, no distraction.

## Brand Personality

Fast. Precise. Professional.

Voice is direct and confident. No hype, no fluff. The product speaks through speed and utility, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with oversized hero metrics, gradient text, and identical icon + heading + text card grids.
- Overly playful or consumer-friendly aesthetics that undermine the professional context of color grading work.
- Crypto / neon-on-black aesthetic. Lutof is a production tool, not a speculative tech project.
- Loud, oversaturated palettes that compete with the color-critical nature of the product's domain.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the first impression.** The page must feel as fast as the product claims to be. Load instantly, scroll smoothly, get to the point.
2. **Precision over personality.** Every pixel, spacing value, and type scale should feel deliberate and tuned. Avoid decorative flourishes that don't serve the message.
3. **Respect the user's eye.** Colorists have trained eyes. The interface must not fatigue them with harsh contrast, clashing hues, or unnecessary motion.
4. **Show, don't tell.** Use the page structure itself to demonstrate the product's clarity. If a section doesn't communicate value in under 3 seconds, cut it.
5. **Dark by default, light if asked.** The primary audience works in dim environments. The default experience should match their ambient context.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as a minimum.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations.
- Ensure sufficient contrast in both dark and light modes.
- Keyboard-navigable download CTA and all interactive elements.
